<p><p>Soil active herbicides are classified into two main types: Synthetic herbicides and Bio-herbicides. Synthetic herbicides are chemically formulated herbicides designed to control weeds in agricultural fields and other vegetation. They are widely used due to their effectiveness in weed control. On the other hand, Bio-herbicides are derived from natural sources such as plants, fungi, or bacteria. They are considered to be more environmentally friendly as they have minimal impact on the ecosystem. Both types of herbicides play a crucial role in weed management in agriculture.</p></p>

<p><p>Soil active herbicides are used in agriculture for weed control at different stages of plant growth. Pre-plant herbicides are applied before planting crops to prevent weed growth. Pre-emergence herbicides are applied after planting but before weed emergence to control weeds as they germinate. Post-emergence herbicides are applied after weed emergence to target actively growing weeds. Each application method targets weeds at specific stages of growth to maximize effectiveness and minimize crop damage.</p></p>
