<p><p>Cassava flour is classified into two main types based on the variety of cassava used in its production: sweet cassava flour and bitter cassava flour. Sweet cassava flour is made from the less toxic variety of cassava, which contains lower levels of cyanogenic compounds, making it safer for consumption. On the other hand, bitter cassava flour is produced from the more toxic variety of cassava, which requires thorough processing to remove the toxins before it can be consumed. Both types of cassava flour have different market demands based on their unique properties and uses.</p></p>
