<p><p>Non-Aqueous Dispersants can be classified into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic dispersants are molecules derived from carbon-based compounds, such as polymers, surfactants, and fatty acids. They are commonly used in industries like paints, coatings, and pharmaceuticals. Inorganic dispersants, on the other hand, are derived from non-carbon compounds like metal oxides, clays, and silicates. They are utilized in applications where a more complex dispersing effect is required, such as in the ceramic and construction industries.</p></p>
