<p><p>Woven geotextiles are commonly categorized based on the material they are made from, with the most common types being polyethylene, polyester, and polyamide. Polyethylene woven geotextiles are known for their high tensile strength and resistance to chemicals, making them ideal for applications in areas with harsh environmental conditions. Polyester woven geotextiles are highly durable and have excellent dimensional stability, making them suitable for use in projects that require long-term stability. Polyamide woven geotextiles are valued for their superior abrasion resistance and high tensile strength, making them popular for applications in high-traffic areas.</p></p>
