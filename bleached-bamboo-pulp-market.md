<p><ul><li>Sulfate Process</li><li>Caustic Soda Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bleached Bamboo Pulp is produced through two main processes - Sulfate Process and Caustic Soda Process. The Sulfate Process involves treating the bamboo with a mixture of chemicals including sodium hydroxide and sodium sulfide, resulting in strong fibers suitable for products like paper and textiles. On the other hand, the Caustic Soda Process uses only caustic soda to break down the bamboo fibers, producing a softer pulp ideal for applications like tissue paper and household items. These two processes cater to different market needs and preferences.</p></p>
