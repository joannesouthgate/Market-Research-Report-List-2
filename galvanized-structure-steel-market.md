<p><p>Galvanized structure steel refers to steel that has been coated with a layer of zinc to protect it from corrosion. There are two main types of galvanized structure steel markets: electrical galvanized structure steel and hot-dip galvanized structure steel. Electrical galvanized steel involves a process of electroplating zinc onto the steel surface, while hot-dip galvanized steel involves immersing the steel in a bath of molten zinc. Both types provide excellent protection against rust and are commonly used in construction and infrastructure projects.</p></p>
