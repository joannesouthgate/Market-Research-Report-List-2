<p><p>Magneto Resistive RAM (MRAM) is a type of non-volatile memory that stores data using magnetic properties. There are two main types of MRAM in the market: Toggle MRAM and STT-MRAM. Toggle MRAM is based on the toggle switch mechanism, where the memory cell switches between two states to store data. On the other hand, STT-MRAM, or Spin Transfer Torque MRAM, uses the spin of electrons to write data. Both types of MRAM offer fast read and write speeds and low power consumption, making them ideal for various applications.</p></p>
