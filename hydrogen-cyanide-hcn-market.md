<p><p>Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN) is primarily produced through two main processes - the Andrussow Process and the Acrylonitrile Process. The Andrussow Process involves the reaction of methane, ammonia, and oxygen over a platinum catalyst, while the Acrylonitrile Process uses propylene, ammonia, and air over a catalyst. The HCN market also includes other processes such as the BMA process and the formamide process. Each process has its own advantages and is used in various industries including pharmaceuticals, plastics, and mining.</p></p>
