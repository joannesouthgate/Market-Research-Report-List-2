<p><p>Marine Scrubbers for Exhaust Gas Cleaning are systems used on ships to reduce emissions of harmful gases such as sulfur oxides (SOx) and nitrogen oxides (NOx) from exhaust gases. These scrubbers work by spraying a liquid solution (usually seawater or alkaline chemicals) into the exhaust stream, which then reacts with the pollutants to neutralize them before they are released into the atmosphere.</p><p>The marine scrubbers market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.4% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing regulations aimed at reducing emissions from ships, such as the IMO 2020 sulfur cap, which has mandated a limit on sulfur content in marine fuels. As ship owners look for ways to comply with these regulations while minimizing operational costs, marine scrubbers have emerged as a cost-effective solution for cleaning exhaust gases.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the marine scrubbers market is the increasing adoption of hybrid scrubber systems, which combine both open-loop and closed-loop scrubbing technologies to provide greater flexibility and efficiency. Additionally, advancements in scrubber designs and materials are also driving market growth, as manufacturers strive to improve the performance and durability of these systems.</p></p>

Both types are essential for reducing maritime emissions.</p></p>
