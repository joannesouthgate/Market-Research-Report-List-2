<p><p>Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) implants typically consist of two main components: the mandibular component and the fossa component. The mandibular component is implanted in the lower jaw, while the fossa component is placed in the skull's temporal bone. The mandibular component allows for smooth movement of the jaw, while the fossa component provides stability and support for the joint. These two components work together to help restore proper function and alleviate pain in patients with TMJ disorders.</p></p>
