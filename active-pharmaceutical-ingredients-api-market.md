<p><p>Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (API) are the components in a drug that produce the desired effect. Synthetic APIs are chemically synthesized and are typically easier and cheaper to manufacture. They are commonly used in traditional pharmaceuticals. Biotech APIs, on the other hand, are derived from living organisms and are used in biotechnology-based drugs. They are more complex to produce but often have higher efficacy and target specific diseases more effectively. Both synthetic and biotech APIs play a crucial role in the pharmaceutical industry, catering to different market demands.</p></p>
