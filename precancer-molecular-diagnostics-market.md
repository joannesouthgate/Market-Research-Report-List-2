<p><p>Precancer molecular diagnostics refer to techniques used to detect genetic mutations or abnormalities that may indicate an increased risk of developing cancer. PCR (polymerase chain reaction) is a method to amplify and detect specific DNA sequences. FISH (fluorescent in situ hybridization) is used to visualize specific DNA sequences in cells. DNA sequencing analyzes the entire genetic code of an individual. Gene chip technology enables the simultaneous analysis of thousands of genes. These techniques play a crucial role in early cancer detection and personalized treatment.</p></p>
