Fixed CSF shunts have a predetermined setting that cannot be changed once implanted, while adjustable CSF shunts allow for modification of flow rates post-implantation. Both types serve the purpose of diverting excess CSF from the brain to another part of the body to alleviate symptoms associated with fluid buildup.</p></p>
