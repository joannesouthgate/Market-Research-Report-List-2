There are two main types of Glucose Dehydrogenase: FAD-Glucose Dehydrogenase and PQQ-Glucose Dehydrogenase. FAD-Glucose Dehydrogenase uses flavin adenine dinucleotide as a cofactor, while PQQ-Glucose Dehydrogenase uses pyrroloquinoline quinone. These enzymes are widely used in biosensors and biofuel cells for glucose detection and energy production.</p></p>
