<p><p>Knee replacement implants, also known as knee prostheses, come in two main types: fixed-bearing implants and mobile-bearing implants. Fixed-bearing implants have a stationary plastic insert that allows the femur to move on the tibia, while mobile-bearing implants have a plastic insert that moves slightly to mimic more natural knee movement. Both types are designed to reduce pain and improve knee function in patients with severe knee damage or arthritis. The market for these implants continues to grow with advancements in technology and an aging population.</p></p>
