<p><p>Hydrogels are classified into two main types: Natural Macromolecule Hydrogel and Synthetic Macromolecule Hydrogel. Natural hydrogels are derived from natural sources such as proteins or polysaccharides, providing biocompatibility and bioactivity. On the other hand, synthetic hydrogels are created through chemical synthesis, offering tunable properties for specific applications. Both types of hydrogels have their advantages and disadvantages, making them suitable for various industries such as healthcare, agriculture, and cosmetics. The choice between natural and synthetic hydrogels depends on the desired properties and applications.</p></p>
