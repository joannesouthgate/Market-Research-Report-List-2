<p><p>Rainwater harvesting is the practice of collecting and storing rainwater for various uses like irrigation, washing, and drinking. Two main types of rainwater harvesting systems are above ground and underground. Above ground systems typically involve collecting rainwater from rooftops and storing it in tanks or barrels. In contrast, underground systems store rainwater in reservoirs located beneath the ground. Both systems are effective in conserving water resources and reducing reliance on municipal water supplies. The choice between the two types depends on factors like space availability and local regulations.</p></p>
