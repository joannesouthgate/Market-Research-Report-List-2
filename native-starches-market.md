<p><p>Native starches are natural starches extracted from plants and used in various industries such as food, paper, and pharmaceuticals. Some of the most commonly used native starches include corn starch, potato starch, cassava starch, wheat starch, and others. Corn starch is widely used in food products for thickening and binding purposes. Potato starch is known for its high clarity and viscosity, making it suitable for applications such as sauces and soups. Cassava starch is used in industries like paper and textiles for its adhesive properties. Wheat starch is commonly used in the food industry for its high gel strength and stable viscosity. Other native starches may include tapioca starch, rice starch, and arrowroot starch, each with their unique characteristics and applications.</p></p>
