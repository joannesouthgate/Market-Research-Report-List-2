<p><p>Semiconductor grade graphite blocks are available in various types such as extruded graphite, molded graphite, and isostatic graphite. Extruded graphite blocks are produced by extruding a mixture of graphite powder and binder through a mold, resulting in a dense and uniform structure. Molded graphite blocks are created by compressing graphite powder into a mold, forming a solid block with good mechanical properties. Isostatic graphite blocks are made using a high-pressure process, resulting in a dense and isotropic graphite material with superior properties for semiconductor applications.</p></p>
