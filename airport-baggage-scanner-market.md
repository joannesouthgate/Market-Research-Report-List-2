<p><p>Airport baggage scanners are commonly categorized into two types- X-ray scanners and Millimeter wave scanners. X-ray scanners use electromagnetic radiation to produce images of the contents of luggage, allowing security personnel to detect any suspicious items. On the other hand, Millimeter wave scanners use radio waves to create a 3D image of the scanned baggage, offering better detection of hidden items. Both types of scanners play a crucial role in enhancing airport security and ensuring the safety of passengers and staff.</p></p>
